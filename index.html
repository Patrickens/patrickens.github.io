About Me Publications CV Blog

I am a computational biologist and machine learning researcher interested in making biological systems more predictable and useful. I work at the interface of mechanistic modeling and modern ML, with a focus on metabolic and process modeling, simulation-based inference, and decision-making under uncertainty.

I completed my PhD in computational biology at ETH Zurich, where I worked on simulation-based metabolic flux inference. My research focused on Bayesian inference of biochemical reaction rates and on developing generative modeling methods for constrained biological systems, giving me a strong foundation in probabilistic modeling and scientific software development.

Most recently, at Differential Bio, I developed differentiable dynamic metabolic simulators and inference pipelines for microbial bioprocesses. In parallel, I built a pipeline for automatic genome-scale metabolic model reconstruction and curation, which led to the company's first commercial software license and is now used to recommend growth media and to screen microbial libraries for commercialization.

Outside of work, I spend a lot of time in the mountains - climbing and ski touring whenever possible. I also enjoy reading and playing guitar, usually alternating between trying to learn something new and just enjoying the process.
